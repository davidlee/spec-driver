## 1. Summary & Context

- **Design Revision**: [DR-127](./DR-127.md)
- **Implementation Plan**: [IP-127](./IP-127.md)
- **Predecessor**: [DE-125](../DE-125-enforce_explicit_module_boundaries_and_coupling_constraints/DE-125.md) — pilot that identified this work
- **Governing Policy**: [POL-003](../../policies/POL-003-module-boundaries.md)
- **Evidence**: [mem.fact.architecture.core-misplaced-modules](../../memory/mem.fact.architecture.core-misplaced-modules.md)

Two modules in `supekku/scripts/lib/core/` are integration hubs misplaced at the
bottom of the architecture. This delta audits every `core/` module, classifies
each into its correct architectural layer, and executes the reclassifications
that unblock honest `spec_driver.core` migration.

## 2. Motivation

`spec_driver.core` is the bottom of the outer layer contract enforced by
import-linter. For that contract to be truthful, the real `core/` modules must
be genuinely foundational — no upward or lateral dependencies.

Today, `supekku/scripts/lib/core/` contains 24 non-test modules. Two of them
violate the layering:

- **`artifact_view.py`** (456 lines) imports 10 registries from 10 different
  domain areas. It's a projection/resolution service that normalises all
  artifact types into a common view model. Consumers: TUI (browser, search,
  widgets), CLI (show). This is orchestration or presentation infrastructure,
  not core.

- **`enums.py`** (111 lines) imports lifecycle status constants from 11 domain
  areas to build a unified enum registry for CLI introspection and validation.
  Consumers: CLI (edit, schema), validator. This is a cross-domain aggregation
  service, not core.

Together they account for 21 cross-area imports from a package that should have
zero. Until they're reclassified, any module that imports from `core/` inherits
their transitive dependency surface, and `spec_driver.core` cannot migrate
honestly.

The remaining 22 modules in `core/` (`paths.py`, `repo.py`, `spec_utils.py`,
`frontmatter_schema.py`, `artifact_ids.py`, `config.py`, etc.) are genuinely
foundational — zero cross-area imports. These are the real core.

## 3. Scope & Objectives

- **Primary Outcomes**:
  - Complete classification of all 24 `core/` modules into their correct
    architectural layer (core / domain / orchestration / presentation).
  - Move `artifact_view.py` to its correct layer.
  - Move `enums.py` to its correct layer (or decompose it).
  - After reclassification, `supekku/scripts/lib/core/` has zero cross-area
    imports, making `spec_driver.core` migration honest.
- **Operational Constraints**:
  - No CLI behaviour changes.
  - Re-export shims preserve existing import paths during transition.
  - Consumer updates can be done incrementally in follow-on work.
- **Dependencies**:
  - DE-125 (completed) — established the migration pattern and contracts.

## 4. Out of Scope

- Migrating the reclassified modules to `spec_driver.*` — this delta
  classifies and moves within the legacy tree or to the new tree, but does not
  attempt to migrate all 22 genuine core modules.
- Reclassifying modules outside `core/` (e.g. `changes/`, `formatters/`).
- Changing the `import-linter` contracts (the existing contracts should continue
  to pass; this delta makes them more truthful, not more restrictive).

## 5. Approach Overview

- **Phase 1: Audit and classify** — inspect every `core/` module, document its
  dependency profile and correct layer, produce a classification table.
- **Phase 2: Reclassify** — move the two misplaced modules to their correct
  locations, add re-export shims, update consumers or leave for follow-on.

### Key Changes

- `artifact_view.py`: likely moves to `spec_driver.orchestration` or a
  dedicated `spec_driver.orchestration.artifact_view` module since it composes
  registries into a unified view.
- `enums.py`: likely decomposes — each domain area already exports its own
  lifecycle constants; the aggregation belongs in orchestration or presentation
  (CLI-facing introspection layer).

## 6. Verification Strategy

- **Architecture Contracts**: `uvx import-linter lint` — both contracts must
  remain passing.
- **Cross-area import count**: after reclassification, `supekku/scripts/lib/core/`
  should have zero cross-area imports (excluding self-references).
- **Test suite**: `uv run pytest supekku spec_driver -x` — full pass.
- **Lint**: `uv run ruff check` — clean.
- **Acceptance Criteria**:
  - Classification table covers all 24 modules.
  - `artifact_view.py` and `enums.py` no longer reside in `core/`.
  - No consumer behaviour changes.

## 7. Risks & Mitigations

- **Risk**: `artifact_view.py` has TUI consumers that depend on fast import
  paths. – _Likelihood_: low – _Impact_: low – _Mitigation_: re-export shim
  preserves the old import path; lazy imports already used by consumers.
- **Risk**: `enums.py` decomposition breaks CLI `edit --status` validation. –
  _Likelihood_: medium – _Impact_: medium – _Mitigation_: keep the unified
  `ENUM_REGISTRY` dict as the public API; only change where it lives, not what
  it returns.
- **Risk**: Reclassification reveals more misplaced modules than the two
  identified. – _Likelihood_: low – _Impact_: low – _Mitigation_: the audit
  phase will catch them; scope expansion is bounded by the 24-module inventory.

## 8. Follow-ups & Tracking

- **Core migration delta**: once `core/` is clean, a follow-on delta can
  migrate the genuine core modules to `spec_driver.core`.
- **Shim retirement**: shims from this delta and DE-125 should be retired in
  the same pass when consumers migrate.
- **`changes/` decomposition**: second-highest coupling hotspot (31 cross-area
  imports); benefits from core being clean first.
